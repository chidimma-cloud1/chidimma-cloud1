# AWS Account Creation

## Overview
Creating an AWS account is the first hands-on step in your cloud computing journey. The process is straightforward and gives you immediate access to the AWS Free Tier 100+ services at no cost for the first 12 months.

## What You Need Before You Start
- A valid email address
- A password for your AWS account
- Personal or business contact details
- A credit or debit card for identity verification
- A phone number for PIN verification

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Go to aws.amazon.com
Click on Create a Free Account on the homepage.

### Step 2: Enter Your Account Details
Provide your email address, create a password, and choose an AWS account name.

### Step 3: Verify Your Email
AWS will send a verification code to your email. Enter the code to confirm and proceed.

### Step 4: Choose Your Account Type
Select Personal or Business. For learning purposes, Personal is fine.

### Step 5: Enter Billing Information
AWS requires a valid credit or debit card for identity verification. You will not be charged as long as you stay within free tier limits.

### Step 6: Identity Verification
AWS will call or send an SMS to your phone number with a PIN code. Enter the PIN to verify your identity.

### Step 7: Select a Support Plan
The basic plan is free and more than sufficient for learning and experimentation.

### Step 8: Access the AWS Management Console
Your central dashboard for accessing and managing all AWS services.

## After Account Creation
- Set up billing alerts under AWS Budgets to monitor spending
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) on your root account
- Explore the AWS Free Tier dashboard to track usage
- Create an IAM user for day-to-day tasks instead of using your root account

## Key Takeaway
Getting started on AWS costs nothing. The free tier gives you 12 months to explore, learn, and build as long as you monitor your usage carefully.
